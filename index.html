Good Scrambled Eggs

Mastering the Simple Classic

    Ingredients

  • NOTE:The sourcing of your eggs is almost as important as how you cook them.
  • Healthy, well-fed and exercised chickens that don't live in stressful, crowded conditions generally produce tastier and healthier eggs.
  • The best (and most expensive!) eggs are from chickens with free access to the outdoors. These eggs are labeled as pasture-raised.
  • Cage free eggs are better than conventionally prodcued eggs, but cage-free chickens can still be housed in large, crowded coops.

  • 3 eggs per person
  • salt
  • pepper to taste
  • butter or cooking oil

Description

Scrambled eggs are seemingly simple to cook, but can be difficult to master. Follow this recipe to produce creamy, delicious eggs everytime.

Directions


  1. Beat the eggs. With a fork, quickly stir the eggs until the yolks are broken and they are uniform yellow.
  2. Add salt and pepper to taste. I've read that you should avoid adding salt before cooking as it denatures the eggs - I've also read you should always add salt for this reason, as it leads to creamier eggs. Experiment!
  3. Melt butter or heat oil in your pan. If you're using a non-stick pan, the fats here are not really needed - they are only for adding flavor.
  4. Cook the eggs over low to medium-low heat. The gentler your heat, the better. The best eggs will be cooked slowly over low heat to prevent overheating.
  5. Stir the eggs frequently to ensure they are cooked evenly. This is key to producing excellent eggs.
  6. Continue stirring. Keep the eggs moving so the heat is evenly distributed.
  7. Once the eggs have reached your desired level of doneness, serve immediately with cheese, creme freche, hot sauce, or plain.
